  - RAM: 8GB
  - SSD: Western Digital WD_BLACK 500GB M.2 NVME
  - HDD: Western Digital 640GB 2.5 SATA
  - Network: TP-Link 5-Port Gigabit Ethernet Switch

# Timeline
Week 1 (February 9, 2026)
  - ✅ Acquire Dell Optiplex 5070 Micro with no storage.
  - Searching through Facebook Marketplace for hard drives.
  - ✅ Found the HDD I'm using now for around 10 bucks two days later.
  - installed the hdd
  - Ordered a TP-Link ethernet port on Amazon, a 7-inch LCD, and 512GB of ssd on eBay!!.
  - The TP-Link came in same-day shipping, while the others are in the process of shipping.
  - Started a sketch of an outline I wanted to design.
    
Week 2 (February 16, 2026)
  - Worked on how to create 3d models
  - Started to print out 3d models at the library.
  - 3D Computer Mount and Ethernet Port Mount completed
  - 7 Inch LCD screen and ssd came in
  - I realize I bought the wrong ssd.
  - Bought the right one on eBay
  - found a place on campus to print out the 3d rack
  - 3d printed handles

Week 3 (February 23, 2026)
  -  The right ssd came in
  -  sold the old one
  -  installed the ssd
  -  messed up the measurements for the monitor mount
  -  starting to actually set up the server pc
  -  installed Ubuntu servers and Casaos
  - trying to make an AI assistant like Jarvis from Iron Man.

Week 4 (March 2, 2026)
  - Went to print everything and was told I had to separate everything into parts, then rebuild it after everything was printed.
  -  Had to rebuild the structure since I had a lot of mistakes happen in the old 3d print.
  - Sent in the print, waiting for it to be printed in week 5.

    
Week 5 (March 9, 2026)
  - It is Tuesday, and my prints are in :)
  - I just realized that the top side of the connectors didn't print, so I have to send another print to get it done
  - I'm supergluing everything together.
  - I downloaded Glances to be able to get a live track of my server on my LCD monitor
  - I relized that my monitor wont be able to fit in the mount i made for it so I just put it against something.
  - The HDMI to display port is holding it up since it is a tight space.
  - Today is Friday, and I am officially done with this project.
  - The connectors didn't print again, but I'm just superglued them on top of the connectors at this point.
